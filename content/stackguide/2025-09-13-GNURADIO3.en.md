## Theoretical Review of AM Modulation

### Basic principle

Amplitude modulation (AM) consists of varying the amplitude of a carrier signal according to the information signal to be transmitted. Mathematically, an AM signal is expressed as:

$$s_{AM}(t) = A_c[1 + m \cdot s_m(t)] \cos(2\pi f_c t)$$

Where:
- $A_c$: Carrier amplitude
- $m$: Modulation index (0 ≤ m ≤ 1)
- $s_m(t)$: Modulating signal (audio)
- $f_c$: Carrier frequency

### Critical parameters

**Modulation index (m)**:
- m = 0: No modulation (pure carrier)
- m = 1: 100% modulation (maximum without distortion)
- m > 1: Overmodulation (distortion)

**Bandwidth**:
The bandwidth of an AM signal is: $BW = 2 \times f_{max}$
For an audio signal up to 5 kHz: BW = 10 kHz

### Advantages and disadvantages

**Advantages**:
- Simple demodulation
- Low-cost receivers
- Robust to phase interference

**Disadvantages**:
- Low energy efficiency (25% theoretical)
- Sensitive to amplitude noise
- Large spectral occupancy

- Block: `QT GUI Range`
- ID: mod_index

### Step 2: Processing blocks

**2.1 Multiplication for modulation**
- Block: `Multiply`
- Connect: Audio Source × mod_index

**2.2 Carrier addition**
- Block: `Add`
- Connect: (Audio × mod_index) + Carrier

**2.3 Final amplitude control**
- Block: `Multiply Const`
- Constant: 0.5 (to avoid saturation)

- Sample Rate: 48000
- Number of Points: 2048
- Connect: Modulator output

**3.2 Spectrum analyzer**

- Sample Rate: 48000
- FFT Size: 2048
- Connect: Modulator output

**3.3 Audio output**
- Block: `Audio Sink`
- Sample Rate: 48000
- Connect: Modulator output

## Testing and Validation of the Modulator

### Test 1: Sinusoidal signal

Temporarily replace the Audio Source with a Signal Source:
- Frequency: 1000 Hz
- Amplitude: 1

**Expected result**:
- Time scope: Carrier with sinusoidal envelope
- Frequency scope: Peak at 10 kHz ± 1 kHz (sidebands)

### Test 2: Modulation index verification

**Procedure**:
1. Set mod_index to 0 → Pure carrier visible
2. Increase to 0.5 → Visible modulation, no distortion
3. Rise to 1.0 → Maximum acceptable modulation
4. Exceed 1.0 → Visible distortion (overmodulation)

**Practical measurements**:
- At m = 0.5: Modulation depth = 50%
- At m = 1.0: Envelope touches zero without exceeding

### Test 3: Spectral analysis

**Complex audio signal**:
- Use real audio source (music)
- Observe spectral spreading around 10 kHz
- Verify sideband symmetry

**Quality metrics**:
- THD (Total Harmonic Distortion) < 1% for m ≤ 0.8
- Signal-to-noise ratio > 40 dB
- Residual carrier suppression

class AM_Tester(gr.top_block):

        # Test signal

## Optimizations and Variants

### Improvement 1: Audio filtering

Add a low-pass filter to limit audio band:

**Audio filter**:
- Block: `Low Pass Filter`
- Sample Rate: 48000
- Cutoff Freq: 5000
- Transition Width: 1000

### Improvement 2: AGC (Automatic Gain Control)

To maintain constant modulation level:

**AGC Block**:
- Block: `AGC`
- Rate: 1e-3
- Reference: 1.0
- Gain: 1.0

### Improvement 3: Double Sideband Suppressed Carrier (DSB-SC)

Remove carrier to improve efficiency:

**Modification**:
- Remove carrier addition
- Keep only multiplication
- Result: DSB-SC signal

### Variant: Synchronous AM

To improve demodulation quality:

**Additions**:

- Selective filtering

## Comparison with Other Techniques

### AM vs FM

| Criteria | AM | FM |
|----------|----|----|
| Energy efficiency | 25% | 100% |
| Receiver complexity | Simple | Complex |
| Audio quality | Average | Excellent |
| Noise resistance | Low | High |
| Bandwidth | 2×f_max | 2×(Δf + f_max) |

### AM vs Digital modulations

**AM advantages**:
- Simple demodulation (envelope detector)
- Compatible with existing receivers
- Reduced cost

**Disadvantages vs digital**:
- Low spectral efficiency
- No error correction
- Limited quality

### Measured performance

## Practical Applications and Limitations

### Real applications

**AM Broadcasting**:
- Commercial AM radio (530-1600 kHz)
- Maritime radio (2-30 MHz)
- Aviation (108-137 MHz)

**Industrial applications**:
- Low-rate data transmission
- Simple telemetry
- Emergency communications

### Technical limitations

**Bandwidth**:
Our 48 kSps modulator limits real RF applications. For real RF:
- Use sample rates > 1 MSps
- Add reconstruction filters
- Integrate RF upconverter

**Dynamic range**:
- 16 bits limit signal-to-noise ratio
- Use high-resolution ADC/DAC for production

**Latency**:
- Audio buffer introduces ~100ms delay
- Optimize for real-time applications

### Production improvements

**Advanced filtering**:
- Precise anti-aliasing filters
- Audio equalization
- Modulation limiters

**Monitoring**:
- Real-time modulation index measurement
- Overmodulation detection
- Continuous spectral analysis

**RF interfacing**:
- Upconverter to radio frequencies
- Power amplifiers
- Emission filters
